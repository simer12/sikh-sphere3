# Akaal Seva App Product Brief

## App Name

**Akaal Seva**

## Simple Description

Akaal Seva is a Sikh spiritual companion app designed to help users read Gurbani, follow their personal Nitnem routine, receive gentle reminders, reflect after prayer, learn Sikh history, and stay connected with daily Sikh practice.

The app is not meant to feel competitive or overwhelming. It should feel calm, respectful, personal, and spiritually supportive.

## What We Are Making

We are building a complete Sikh practice ecosystem.

The app helps users:

- Read daily Nitnem Banis
- Create their own personal prayer schedule
- Choose what Banis they read in the morning, afternoon, evening, and night
- Receive soft reminders at the times they choose
- Track reading progress without pressure
- Build gentle streaks and consistency
- Earn meaningful milestone badges
- Save reflections after reading
- Read daily Hukamnama
- Listen to live Kirtan
- Explore Sikh history
- Use a Sikh calendar
- Save bookmarks and reading history
- Personalize the app experience

The goal is to support a Sikh user’s daily spiritual rhythm.

## Core Vision

Akaal Seva should become a daily companion for Sikhs around the world.

Instead of only showing religious content, the app should help users build a living relationship with their practice.

The app should ask:

> What Banis do you read daily?
> When do you usually read them?
> Would you like a gentle reminder?
> What stayed with you after reading today?

This turns the app from a content library into a personal spiritual routine builder.

## Target Users

The app is for:

- Sikhs who read Nitnem daily
- Sikhs who want to become more consistent
- Young Sikhs learning their daily Banis
- Families who want a simple spiritual companion
- Users who want Gurbani, Hukamnama, Kirtan, history, and calendar features in one place
- Beginners who need structure and gentle guidance
- Practicing Sikhs who want a clean, respectful digital Gutka-style experience

## User Experience Feeling

The app should feel:

- Calm
- Respectful
- Spiritual
- Encouraging
- Private
- Non-competitive
- Easy to use
- Beautiful but not distracting
- Built for daily use

The app should never make users feel guilty. It should encourage them gently.

Examples of the tone:

- “Continue when ready.”
- “A short prayer today is enough.”
- “Your rhythm is building.”
- “Still time today.”
- “Come back gently when it feels right.”

## Main App Sections

## 1. Home

The Home screen is the daily spiritual dashboard.

It shows:

- App identity
- Daily inspiration
- Today’s Nitnem schedule
- Progress for each routine
- Weekly rhythm card
- Current streak
- Latest milestone badge
- Quick access to major features

The Home screen should help users understand:

- What they planned to read today
- What they have completed
- What remains
- Where to continue

## 2. My Daily Nitnem

This is one of the most important features.

Users can build their own daily Nitnem schedule.

They can create routines for:

- Morning
- Afternoon
- Evening
- Night

For each routine, users can:

- Choose which Banis they read
- Set a reminder time
- Enable or disable the routine
- Choose reminder style
- Adjust their routine anytime

Example routine:

Morning Nitnem at 5:00 AM:

- Japji Sahib
- Jaap Sahib
- Tav-Prasad Savaiye
- Chaupai Sahib
- Anand Sahib

Evening Rehras at 6:30 PM:

- Rehras Sahib

Night Sohila at 9:30 PM:

- Kirtan Sohila

The goal is to let each user shape the app around their actual practice.

## 3. Nitnem Banis

The Nitnem section lets users browse and read daily Banis.

Current Banis include:

- Japji Sahib
- Jaap Sahib
- Tav-Prasad Savaiye
- Chaupai Sahib
- Anand Sahib
- Rehras Sahib
- Sohila Sahib

The reading experience includes:

- Gurmukhi text
- Progress while reading
- Completion marks
- “Done today” status
- Reading streaks
- Reflection prompt after completion

The reading experience should feel like a calm digital Gutka.

## 4. Reading Progress

Users can see their progress in a gentle way.

Progress includes:

- Percent read for each Bani
- Completed today marks
- Number of completions
- Daily streak
- Weekly goal progress
- Routine completion progress

The progress system should motivate without pressure.

Instead of leaderboards, the app uses personal consistency.

## 5. Weekly Goals

The app supports gentle weekly goals.

Example:

> Complete 3 prayers this week.

This is intentionally soft. The goal is not to make the user feel behind. It is to help them return to practice.

## 6. Milestone Badges

Users can earn meaningful spiritual consistency badges.

Examples:

- First full Japji Sahib
- First full Rehras Sahib
- First full Sohila Sahib
- 7 days of Japji Sahib
- 7 day Nitnem rhythm

Badges should feel like quiet encouragement, not game rewards.

## 7. Reflection Prompts

After completing a Bani, the app asks a gentle reflection question.

Example:

> What is one thought, feeling, or line you want to carry from this reading?

Users can:

- Write a short reflection
- Skip the reflection
- Save private notes

The goal is to turn reading into a deeper habit, not just a checkbox.

## 8. Reading History

The Reading History section shows:

- What the user read
- When they read it
- Total reads
- Current streak
- Reading minutes
- Unique Banis read
- Recent reflections
- Milestone badges

This helps users look back on their journey.

## 9. Daily Hukamnama

The app includes Daily Hukamnama.

Users can read the Hukamnama with:

- Gurmukhi
- English translation
- Punjabi translation
- Reference information
- Share option
- Refresh option

This helps users stay connected with the daily Hukamnama.

## 10. Live Kirtan

The app includes a Live Kirtan section.

Users can listen to live Kirtan streams from Gurdwaras.

The purpose is to help users feel spiritually connected throughout the day.

## 11. Sikh History

The app includes Sikh history content.

Users can explore:

- Guru history
- Sikh eras
- Important events
- Historical articles
- Guru Nanak Dev Ji life stories

This makes the app not only a prayer app, but also a learning companion.

## 12. Dasam Granth

The app includes Dasam Granth content.

Users can browse compositions and read available content in the app.

## 13. Sikh Calendar

The app includes Sikh calendar features.

Users can see:

- Gurpurabs
- Shaheedi Divas
- Sangrand
- Masia
- Puranmasi
- Historical Sikh events

This helps users stay aware of important Sikh dates.

## 14. Bookmarks

Users can bookmark content they want to return to.

Bookmarks help users save:

- Favorite Banis
- Important readings
- Meaningful content

## 15. Preferences

Users can personalize the app.

Preferences include:

- Dark mode
- Font size
- Language preference
- Audio autoplay setting
- Download preferences

The app should support different reading comfort levels.

## 16. Notifications And Reminders

The app supports reminders.

Reminder style should be gentle.

Users can choose:

- Silent mode
- Gentle reminder
- Standard reminder with one follow-up

Examples of reminders:

- “A quiet moment for Japji Sahib, when you are ready.”
- “Your evening Rehras reminder is here.”
- “Still here for you. Continue only if this is a good moment.”

The app should avoid spam and guilt-based reminders.

## Main User Journey

## First-Time User Journey

1. User opens the app.
2. User sees onboarding.
3. User reaches Home.
4. App invites them to shape their daily Nitnem.
5. User opens My Daily Nitnem.
6. User chooses Banis for morning, afternoon, evening, and night.
7. User selects reminder times.
8. User starts reading from their schedule.
9. User completes a Bani.
10. App asks for a reflection.
11. App updates progress, streaks, and routine status.

## Daily User Journey

1. User opens Home.
2. User sees Today’s Nitnem schedule.
3. User taps Morning Nitnem.
4. User reads one or more Banis.
5. User marks them complete.
6. Routine progress updates.
7. Weekly rhythm updates.
8. User may write a reflection.
9. Later, user gets a gentle reminder for Rehras or Sohila.

## Long-Term User Journey

Over time, the app helps users:

- Build consistency
- Understand their routine
- Reflect more deeply
- Read more regularly
- Stay connected with Gurbani
- Learn Sikh history
- Follow Sikh calendar events
- Feel spiritually supported

## What Makes This App Different

Akaal Seva is not just a Gurbani reader.

It combines:

- Gurbani reading
- Personal Nitnem scheduling
- Soft reminders
- Habit support
- Spiritual reflection
- Sikh history
- Daily Hukamnama
- Kirtan
- Calendar
- Personal progress

The unique idea is that the app adapts to the user’s real Sikh practice.

## What The App Should Avoid

The app should avoid:

- Competitive leaderboards
- Harsh streak loss language
- Guilt-based notifications
- Overly flashy gamification
- Cluttered UI
- Distracting visuals during Gurbani reading
- Treating prayer like a productivity task

The app should always respect the sacred nature of Gurbani.
