# Sikh History Feature

## Overview
A comprehensive Sikh history module that covers four major eras from 1469 to present, with properly cited scholarly sources.

## Structure

### Four Historical Eras:

1. **The Age of the Gurus (1469-1708)**
   - Guru Nanak Dev Ji - The Founder
   - The Ten Sikh Gurus
   - Guru Gobind Singh Ji and the Khalsa

2. **The Warrior Era (1708-1849)**
   - Banda Singh Bahadur
   - The Sikh Misls - Warrior Confederacy
   - The Sikh Empire under Maharaja Ranjit Singh
   - The Anglo-Sikh Wars

3. **Modern Sikh History (1849-1984)**
   - Sikhs Under British Rule
   - Sikhs in Independent India
   - The Global Sikh Diaspora

4. **1984 and Its Aftermath**
   - Operation Blue Star (with content warning)
   - 1984 Anti-Sikh Violence (with content warning)
   - Aftermath and Healing
   - Sikh Diaspora Impact

## Sources Used

All content is based on these scholarly sources:

1. **Sikh History From Persian Sources** - JS Grewal & Irfan Habib
2. **Contemporary Sources of Sikh History (1469-1708)** - Dr Ganda Singh
3. **History of Sikhism** - Joseph Davey Cunningham (1903)
4. **The History of the Sikhs** - Hari Ram Gupta
5. **Panth Prakash** - Rattan Singh Bhangu (1841)
6. **Never Ever We Forget Our Martyrs – Genocide Nov 1984** (Archive.org)
7. **The Digital Story of 1984** - S. Devgan
8. **Report of the Nanavati Commission** - Ministry of Home Affairs, India
9. **Sikhs Outside Punjab** - UNHCR/RefWorld
10. **SikhiWiki** - Online Sikh Encyclopedia
